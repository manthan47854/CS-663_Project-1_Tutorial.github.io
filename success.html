Successes and Failures

Where it shines

  • Controlled sessions: fixed tripod or wall mount, single athlete centered, consistent backdrop, known capture zone (tape box on floor), and minimal bystanders.
  • Repeatable drills: clear start/end (sprint starts, squats, CMJ, depth jumps, medicine-ball throws); scripted sets & reps for easy segmentation.
  • Short, explosive actions: tasks with strong sport-specific priors (pitching, serves, golf swings, Olympic lifts) that benefit from temporal templates.
  • On-device feedback loops: instant rep counts, ROM/angle checks, depth/hit detection, cadence/tempo, asymmetry flags; audio/visual cues within <100 ms perceived latency.
  • Predictable environments: indoor gyms, cages, labs; static lighting; uncluttered backgrounds or solid backdrops that improve foreground separation.
  • High-quality capture: fast shutter and higher frame rates when available (60–240 fps); fixed exposure & white balance; low compression.
  • Simple kinematics planes: sagittal or frontal-plane motions where 2D keypoints map well to coaching cues without full 3D reconstruction.
  • Lightweight edge models: quantized runtimes on phones, tablets, or small edge boxes for low-latency inference and privacy-preserving workflows.
  • Wearable/IMU fusion: combining a single phone/camera with wrist/waist IMUs to stabilize yaw/roll and enrich phase detection.
  • Pre-session calibration: checkerboard or ArUco quick-check; known distances/heights; floor markers to bound athlete distance (2–6 m sweet spot).
  • Apparel & contrast: fitted, high-contrast clothing (no baggy layers); shoes distinct from flooring; optional lightweight markers/tape for key joints.
  • Coaching stations: combines, team warm-ups, youth clinics; repeated stations with identical camera geometry boost accuracy and comparability.
  • Rehab & return-to-play: baseline snapshots, weekly deltas, side-by-side video overlays, and auto-generated session summaries.
  • Offline batching: real-time cues during drills with richer summaries post-session (aggregate best reps, variability, trend charts).
  • Data stewardship: on-device processing with optional anonymization (face blur, jersey blur) to satisfy privacy expectations without cloud upload.

Failure modes & mitigations

  • Occlusion/crowding: elevate camera; widen FOV; floor lanes; stagger athletes; use bottom-up multi-person models with soft-NMS; enable ID re-association.
  • Rapid rotations/spin: fuse IMU gyros; shorten exposure (~1/1000s if possible); multi-view capture for disambiguation; gyro-stabilized or fixed mounts.
  • Blur/lighting: add continuous lighting; lock exposure/WB; avoid LED flicker (choose flicker-free 100/120 Hz fixtures); prevent auto-exposure hunting; mild denoise in post.
  • Sync drift (video↔IMU/multi-cam): periodic clap/LED sync; log NTP offsets; prefer hardware genlock/time-code when available; record per-device timebase.
  • Scale/distance changes: keep athlete within 2–6 m; prefer primes over heavy zoom; auto-recentering and dynamic crop; re-estimate focal length if zoom changes.
  • Frame drops/overheating: cap to 1080p@60–120 fps; enable hardware acceleration; wired power; local storage (not Wi-Fi streams); monitor thermals.
  • Domain shift (drills→games): augment with clutter/occlusions; venue-specific fine-tuning; confidence gating & fallback heuristics; flag “out-of-distribution” frames.
  • Reflective/complex backgrounds: portable backdrop/scrim; polarizing filter for glare; background blur/subtraction; avoid mirrors/windows behind subject.
  • Calibration drift: quick daily check (2–3 poses or a checkerboard); store per-lens distortion profiles; re-run any time the mount moves.
  • Edge-of-frame truncation: enforce safe margins; visual reticle to keep head/feet inside ROI; auto-pan recenter prompts.
  • Rolling-shutter wobble: prefer global-shutter cams where possible; raise shutter speed; avoid aggressive electronic stabilization that warps geometry.
  • Apparel pitfalls: loose clothing, long jerseys, or matching floor colors hide joints—encourage contrasty fitted gear; optional joint tape/markers for bootstrapping.
  • Adverse weather/particulates: rain/snow/dust on lens; use hood/hydrophobic coat; wipe checks; relocate under cover; raise ISO with care to control noise.
  • Motion aliasing (low fps): below ~60 fps, very fast limbs strobe; increase fps or infer sub-frame motion via optical flow; warn when capture is insufficient.
  • Small, fast objects (balls/bats): high-speed camera for impact events; predictive tracking; fuse with radar/IMU when precision on contact timing matters.
  • Multi-athlete ID swaps: persistent tracklets with appearance embeddings; jersey/bib color tags; assign lanes; lock ID to ROI when possible.
  • Camera tilt/level drift: bubble level during setup; draw horizon grid; auto-estimate ground plane and correct angles in software.
  • Storage/power hiccups: preflight check (free space, write speed); high-endurance cards; rolling file segments to limit corruption; UPS or battery pack.
  • Privacy/compliance: face/jersey blur in-app; opt-in consents; on-device only by default; minimal metadata retention; coach-controlled export.
